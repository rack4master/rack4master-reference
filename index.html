7-Band Parametric EQ

Shapes the frequency balance of the target to match the reference. SUB and AIR are shelf filters; the 5 middle bands are bell-shaped (±12 dB).

SUB — 50 Hz (shelf)
Sub-bass weight and rumble. Boost adds depth; cut cleans up low-end mud.
BASS — 100 Hz
Body and punch. The warmth foundation of the mix.
LO-MID — 300 Hz
Warmth vs. muddiness zone. Often cut slightly for clarity.
MID — 1 kHz
Presence and forward character. Defines attack and intelligibility.
HI-MID — 3 kHz
Clarity, edge, and harshness. Use with care — very audible range.
PRESENCE — 8 kHz
Brilliance, definition, and consonant detail.
AIR — 16 kHz (shelf)
Open, airy shimmer. Subtle boost lifts the top end.

Dynamics Processor

Feed-forward compressor that reduces the dynamic range to match the reference crest factor and perceived density.

Threshold
Level above which gain reduction begins. Lower = more compression starts earlier.
Ratio
Compression intensity. 2:1 = gentle; 8:1 = heavy; 20:1 = near-limiting.
Attack
How fast compression kicks in after a transient. Slow = punchy transients pass through.
Release
How fast gain returns after the signal drops. Too fast causes audible pumping.
Makeup Gain
Compensates the volume lost through compression. Keep the output around 0 dBFS.

Harmonics & Filter

High-pass filter removes low-frequency rumble. Saturation adds harmonic coloration. Both run before the dynamics stage.

HPF Frequency
Cutoff point. Everything below is rolled off at 24 dB/oct (4th-order — twice as steep as typical). Derived from sub-bass energy delta vs. reference.
HPF Resonance (Q)
Slight resonant emphasis just before the cutoff. At Q > 1 you hear a subtle bump — useful to compensate for the filter's energy loss.
Saturation
Soft-clip waveshaper at 4× oversampling. Adds even + odd harmonics for analog warmth. 0 % = transparent; 100 % = heavy coloration. Derived from spectral flatness difference.

Output Stage

M/S matrix adjusts stereo image width and phase balance. Limiter ensures the signal never exceeds the ceiling. Output Gain sets the final export level.

Mid Gain
Adjusts the center (mono-compatible) component — kick, bass, lead vocals. Boost to push center elements forward.
Side Gain
Adjusts stereo width. Boost to widen; cut to focus the image. Negative values narrow toward mono.
Limiter Ceiling
Absolute peak ceiling (brick wall, 20:1, 1 ms attack, 150 ms release). No sample can exceed this value after processing.
Output Gain
Final level trim applied after the limiter, before export. Use to match target loudness or create headroom.
